Methodology

Designed to make confident mistakes harder.

The process separates official product evidence, statistical review patterns, user allegations, interpretation and recommendation.

Research loopReview analysisConfidenceAdversarial auditLimitations
01 · Research loop

Frame, discover, evaluate, challenge, publish.

The research began with a locked protocol defining the decision, audience, scope, inclusion rules and evidence standards. India and Global product surfaces were kept separate. Official product, legal and technical material was used to establish what Delta documents; third-party and user sources were used to find contradictions and possible failure modes.

01

Define the audience, decision, boundaries and falsifiable questions before searching.

02

Map official, regulatory, technical, competitive and user evidence.

03

Evaluate

Assess directness, freshness, independence, bias risk and exact citation fit.

02 · Review intelligence

Calculate first. Interpret second.

The supplied Google Play dataset contained 22,199 unique review IDs from April 2024 through July 2026. Deterministic analysis calculated ratings, text duplication, time trends, version outcomes, helpful votes, reply coverage and response delay.

Meaningful negative reviews were classified through a transparent multi-label taxonomy, then checked against unsupervised TF-IDF and NMF topic modelling. The model independently surfaced fee, chart/lag, support/payment, fraud-language and order/stop-loss clusters, increasing confidence in the dominant structure.

Important: helpful votes measure engagement, not truth. App reviews identify candidate failure modes and user language; they cannot prove platform causation or incident prevalence.
03 · Confidence language

Confidence measures evidence—not conviction.

High

Direct and reproducible

Exact dataset calculations or clearly documented official product behaviour.

Medium-high

Strong pattern, important limits

Multiple aligned signals with plausible rival explanations still remaining.

Medium

Useful hypothesis

A defensible opportunity that requires internal data, testing or implementation discovery.

Low / rejected

Not publishable as fact

Anecdotal, contradicted or unsupported by a comparable and representative evidence base.

04 · Adversarial audit

The leading conclusions were attacked before publication.

The final audit applied five tests: citation entailment, source independence, rival explanations, language calibration and falsifiability. It downgraded withdrawal recovery and order-intent guardrails, narrowed reliability claims and rejected claims that reviews prove manipulation, widespread failures or comparative leadership.

Recommendations are therefore presented as experiments with measurable uncertainty—not as a roadmap Delta should blindly adopt.

05 · Limitations

What public evidence cannot tell us.

No authenticated Delta account, order history, support tickets, withdrawal-state records, internal telemetry, incident postmortems or representative interviews were available. Public documentation establishes what Delta states or exposes, not how every workflow performs in production.

The highest-value next evidence would be anonymised ticket taxonomy, event-log completeness, fee-comprehension tests, verified liquidation cases, maintenance exposure outcomes, API-safety telemetry and representative task-based research.
